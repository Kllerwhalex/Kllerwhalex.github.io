Day 1, Life, Time and Control – A examination of how to live life.

 

Reasoning

  • Life has three periods: the past, the present and the future. We live in the present and therefore only exert control over present. The past is certain, while the future is doubtful.
  • Our control is limited, we cannot control the weather or the earth. We have limited control over others, our body possession and status. But what we do have is complete control over is actualization of our mind. Specifically our beliefs, emotions, impulses, desires and aversion, from which all of our action stems. We could reason/convince in and out the existence of beliefs, emotions, impulses, desires and aversion. For example, mental illness. TV, Pornography and games in one form or another is all simply the reasoning of the mind. It is all in the mind.
  • An impulse, emotion, desires, aversion and belief not acted on, not materialized, is merely a thought, it is nonexistence.
  • The exertion of this control we have on the mind is referred to as self-control, it is the control of our beliefs, emotions, fear, impulses, desires and aversion.
  • Life is then only in the present, not in the future or the past. For all then, the primary experience is the same, as we only experience the present.
  • All humans want a good life, as I assume all humans do.  I assume, from logical conclusion, that everyone then wants the best life possible. As it what is the BEST in their opinion.
  • To do so then, you must utilize the short present in the most efficient fashion possible to achieve this BEST present. Simply, you want to receive the most you can from that fleeting moment. The more, the better. (you exert control over what you can to guide you to what you want)
  • Then to live the best present. Four major virtues(crystals of principles) follows:
  • Self-Control – When it is appropriate we must act w/ self-control. This is what allows us to immerse us fully into what is the present, allowing us to concentrate it, to extract the wonders of it. This is also what prevents (supress (ST), understand, reason (LT)) other distraction, emotions, ideas, thoughts, and impulses from disrupting our immersion. This is what allows us to use wisdom to make choices, the best choices. The practice of self-control is simply to act with self-control, this closely ties with wisdom.
  • Wisdom – We must choose our activities. Also a part of our logic, it assists us in self-control through logic and knowledge. It is the part of you that thinks and decides what the best is and why is it the best action. In my ideals, you can also call such “best”, a meaningful or purposeful action. The practice of wisdom is to always maintain self-control over oneself so that you are always practicing wisdom, thinking and making the best choices and preventing yourself from making poor ones.
  • Courage – We must face all circumstances with courage. Courage is not merely the absence of fear. In fact, it only occurs in the presence of fear, doubt and all other horrors. It is two parts, one is the self-control that despite the overwhelming fear, controlling yourself to calmly and wisely understand, evaluate and proceed through an action. Two, is the unwavering belief that you can accomplish the first. This is what allows us to self-control ourselves to utilize wisdom to make wise decision in difficult situations. This maintains the constancy of Self Control and Wisdom to make the best present.
  • Justice – Part wisdom, part self-control. We must be just towards others. We must be just towards ourselves. This is what prevents us from falling into difficult situations in society. Two parts. Self-control to allow us to utilize wisdom while not performing damaging actions. While wisdom for us to long term be free of performing the damaging action.
  • In short, for the best
  • Self-control for ability for possibilities(rather than controlled by nature)
  • Wisdom for choosing the best(rather than controlled by nature)
  • Courage for maintaining self-control and wisdom in difficulty
  • Justice for preventing one from falling into difficulty
  • --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • So live in the present with self-control, wisdom, courage and justice, and you will have the best. That is the best that even the gods can ask of you
  • Ask all of the following questions upon every decision. You must have yes for all four.
  • Am I living with self-control? If not how can I?
  • Am I living with wisdom? If not how can I?
  • Am I living with courage? If not how can I?
  • Am I living with justice? If not how can I?

With two machines working, reason and emotion which reads the situation and your own belief to output its own action. A good analogy would be relating the idea of a belief to pieces of code.   • On a reaction level: We cannot control instinct. We can manipulate its outcome in the momentary level. We can change its programming in the long term, but on the reaction level, we can’t control it.

 

  • So belief. What is belief then?

  • So to change how we act on a momentary basis, we must first change our belief.
  • Our emotion and reason is based upon our belief, if there is any problem check back to your belief.

Duty.
Life is inherently meaningless. A chaos of particles.
I choose my role in this universe.
In choosing this role, I gain what is called duty.
Duty is the role; the role is the duty. They are two inseparable side of the same coin.
The duty is what makes the role meaningful, while meaningfulness is why we choose the roles, why we perform the duty.
A teacher who does not teach is not a teacher.
A student who does not learn is not a student.
A human who does not act as a human is not one.
That is what we call duty. After all a role is a set of action, a duty is exactly that set.

  • The part of being not unhappy. Not worried or being punished by yourself.

Our worries and our roles are the only two things that makes us unhappy.
Being focused without any other reason makes us happy.
That is happiness.
